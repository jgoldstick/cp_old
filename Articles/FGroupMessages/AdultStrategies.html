Adult Strategies (Adult Child Relationship Manual) -- Dr. Jim MacDonald

BALANCE: Develop an easy give-and-take where each of you takes turns and do as much as the other

Decrease (do less):

  • Fails to wait for child.
  • Acts much more than the child.
  • Communicates much more than child.
  • Makes all the choices and decisions.
  • Dominates the turns.
  • Allows child to dominate.
  • Initiates more than responds.
  • Responds more than intiates.
  • Allows child to leave interactions.
Increase (do more):
  • Waits for child to take his turn.
  • Acts only as much as the child.
  • Communicates as much as the child.
  • Shares choices and decisions.
  • Shares turns with child.
  • Ensures child does not dominate.
  • Initiates as much as responds.
  • Responds as much as initiates.
  • Keeps child for a few more turns.

MATCH: Help child learn by acting and communicating in ways he can try to do

Decrease (do less):
  • Acts in ways child cannot do.
  • Plays in ways child cannot do.
  • Plays role of observer.
  • Acts in ways unrelated to child's activitiy.
  • Talks more than plays with child.
  • Communicates outside child's experience.
  • Expects child to do impossible.
  • Expects child to do less than he can.
  • Overstimulates the child.
  • Understimulates the child.
Increase (do more):
  • Acts in ways child can do.
  • Plays in ways child can do.
  • Plays role of partner.
  • Acts in ways related to child's activity.
  • Shows child new ways to play.
  • Plays as much as talks.
  • Communicates about child's experience.
  • Expects child to do what is possible.
  • Expects child to do his capacity.
  • Reduces overstimulation.
  • Provides effective stimulation.

RESPONSIVENESS: Responds to child's emerging/productive behaviors, not immature or negative behaviors.

Decrease (do less):
  • Ignores child's actions.
  • Ignores child's communications.
  • Ignores child's interests.
  • Responds in adult ways.
  • Responds more to immature behavior.
  • Responds to negative/disruptive behavior.
  • Stimulates more than child can handle.
  • Respond critically.
  • Responds to old communications.
  • Responds so that child leaves.
Increase (do more):
  • Responds to child's actions.
  • Responds to child's communications.
  • Responds to child's interests.
  • Responds in child-like ways.
  • Responds more to mature behavior.
  • Ignores negative/disruptive behavior.
  • Stimulates child appropriately.
  • Responds with acceptance.
  • Responds more to new communications.
  • Responds so child stays interacting.

SHARING CONTROL WITH CHILD: Allow adult and child to share control of interaction.

Decrease (do less):
  • Controls agenda or activity.
  • Ignores child's lead.
  • Follows child's lead too much.
  • Allows child to control interaction.
  • Does too much for the child.
  • Excessively questions or tests the child.
  • Prevents the child from having his say.
  • Provides few or no boundaries.
  • Prevents child having effect on you.
  • Allows the child no independence.
  • Allows child to not cooperate.
Increase (do more):
  • Shares agenda or activity with child.
  • Follows child's lead.
  • Shares the lead with the child.
  • Requires child to allow you to interact.
  • Allows child to do for self.
  • Comments more than questions.
  • Allows child to have his say.
  • Provides clear boundaries.
  • Allows child to have effects on you.
  • Allows child reasonable independence.
  • Requires child to cooperate.

EMOTIONAL ATTACHMENT: Establish relationship that is playful and enjoyable for adult and child.

Decrease (do less):
  • Makes interactions tense/ stressful.
  • Criticizes and judges child.
  • Interacts in rigid ways.
  • Acts in formal and distant style.
  • Keeps physical distance.
  • Ignores or undoes child's attempts.
  • Shows serious syle with little humor.
  • Fails to keep child interacting.
  • Provides little warmth and comfort.
  • Expresses little or no affection.
Increase (do more):
  • Accepts and enjoys the child.
  • Interacts in flexible ways.
  • Acts in informal and personal style.
  • Interacts with contact and closeness.
  • Supports and enhances child's attempts.
  • Shows animated, humorous style.
  • Regularly keeps child interacting.
  • Provides consistent warmth and comfort.
  • Expresses regular and genuine affection.
